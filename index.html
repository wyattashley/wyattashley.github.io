2022 FIRST Robotics

Drive Train

Launcher

Intake

 In the realm of robotics and mechatronics, the Swerve Chassis represents a significant innovation that seamlessly combines precision, agility, and versatility. This technology incorporates four rotational and translational axes, introducing complexity when configuring the forward and reverse kinematic equations.

 My primary responsibility within the project was to develop these essential equations, ensuring that with just three input commands (left/right, forward/reverse, and turning), all four wheels operate in harmony. This experience underscores my capability to address intricate engineering challenges and contribute to the advancement of robotics and mechatronics.

 For this particular season, our team made the strategic decision to incorporate a two-wheel launcher into our setup, offering us the capability to apply precise amounts of spin to the game ball. In my role, I took charge of prototyping, contributed to the design process, and successfully programmed the control system responsible for launching the ball.

 By implementing variable spin and adjustable launch distances, I was able to create an adaptive launcher that proved to be highly effective in scoring the ball from any position on the field. This project highlights my skills in both hardware development and programming.

 In light of the game piece being a sizable ball, our team collectively opted for a two-stage compliant wheel intake mechanism. In the initial stage, the ball was taken in by our primary intake system, after which it proceeded to a wheeled condenser. This secondary stage was responsible for positioning the ball more predictably, preparing it for a more accurate launch.

 This decision demonstrates thoughtful approach to game strategy and ability to devise a well-coordinated, multi-stage solution to address the challenges posed by the game piece.
